You might be wondering in what sense I have kept this title? But right now this is what is going on everywhere. Millions of people are getting betrayed by the social media and companies and we just don’t care. Internet is big free playground you might say but what is really going behind the scenes is different story today.

Today on almost every site you see those “Click on I agree” boxes. Blindly you just share your privacy with them. …. Like wait what are you trying to say? You must have heard about Facebook Cambridge Analytica scandal which exposed almost 87 million profiles for Trump campaign in 2016. If that’s not enough then on September 25, 2018 another potential data leak of 50 million users happened. And this is not related to Facebook only, Amazon, Google, Netflix, etc. are also involved in this privacy related issues.

You just blindly click on “I agree” to the terms and conditions every time. There’s no denying that terms and conditions are long and complex; no one wants to read through 30 pages of fine print. But the internet is nowhere near to make sense to your privacy.

>“Arguing that you don’t care about the right to privacy because you have nothing to hide, is no different than saying you don’t care about free speech because you have nothing to say.” -Edward Snowden

Google already has ecosystem which harnesses your data daily each second a day. From what time you wake up to whom you send message and to where you go to work. Everything is available on internet about you even more than you know about yourself. You might say, okay I don’t use social media that means my privacy is safe with me. But may be you’re wrong.

Millions of puppy photos used for what purpose? Maybe to train machine which will get smarter. Your location history is attracting investors by which shopping stores you go. Watching a movie online, going to restaurants using app, food delivery apps, etc everything is generating data which is to be treasured by this big corporations.

Your data is more than anything to this web world- it powers today’s big corporations with profits. Everyday the companies that you don’t even know collect information about you.

### What constitutes of “Personal Data” ?

![alt](/blog/2019-03-04/facebook-privacy.png)

The internet might seem like one big privacy nightmare. Your health records, personal security numbers, your banking details, even your flight booked tickets which you throw after flight contains plenty of data. And this big corporations bid for your data. Highest bidders get most out of it.

Often it is clearly mention that information is being collected but not viewed or is buried somewhere between 30 page terms and conditions. In each of the scenario user gets something in return that is to use the app which you called free, to buy latest footwear. You use there social media apps such as Instagram, Messenger or Maps which don’t cost you money. You pay with your personal data which is then targeted towards you with hell lot of ads.

### Cybersecurity and You

Not a day goes by news related to privacy or data leaks. To put simply, privacy and security are converging. Nobody is safe; everyone is vulnerable to biometric, cloud, phishing attacks to steal your valuable data. In Jan, 2019 a security researcher Troy Hunt found almost 770 million accounts and passwords and it was openly available on the hacking sites.

The number of data we create daily is astounding- 2.5 quintillion bytes..! And it is increasing day by day- byte by byte. Surely this much is more than enough for hackers to steal. Though new rules and regulations related to data and cybersecurity have been implemented after so much that has happened over years still consumers have to understand the basics principles of not getting fooled by phishing sites, mails.

### The Future of Personal Data Collection

Okay, So you might be scared right now. If only computers and smartphones can cause this privacy nightmare then be ready for next generation gadgets, smartwatches, smart speakers, censors embedded wears, facial recognition enabled surveillance cameras and gadgets that you find weird. In a sense this future is already has begun.

As scientists are pushing the limits of what’s possible with AI there is no denying of the carelessness for assuming an AI for real person in near future. This new Algorithms are already generating “fake” data to train machines and AI.

The website called [thispersondoesnotexist](https://thispersondoesnotexist.com/) is an example of creating faces of fake people which you might say exist but in reality they don’t. This kind of AI’s can be used to create an imaginary person with your data which doesn’t even exist. This way try to imagine you’re being matched with person on tinder or person you followed on Instagram actually exist. Your personal data is being analyzed each second on variety of levels to take important decisions.

### Conclusion

Tech companies are also beginning to acknowledge that personal data collection needs to be regulated. The overflow of privacy scandals that have come to light in recent years- from Facebook Cambridge Analytica to Google’s shady location tracking- users still don’t understand how their data is being used or shared, traded. Before we can understand the future of personal data collection we need to understand the present state. Until consumers understand the ecosystem of internet there is no denying that some fishy scandals will occure in near future.
