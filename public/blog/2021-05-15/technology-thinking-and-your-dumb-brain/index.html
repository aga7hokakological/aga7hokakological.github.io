Technology, thinking and your dumb brain

15 May 2021 • 4 min read

“Thinking is difficult, that’s why most people judge.” - Carl Jung

I don’t know if this quote is relevant for this post or not but one thing I know for sure is that technology has changed our way of thinking in every way. 

What is thinking?

Thinking is process of consciously using the brain and responding to the world from your reactions to it. Your thinking frame of reference is different from others. It is defined by point of view or perspective, a frame of reference is shaped by our prior knowledge, assumptions values or language, about others. Our frame of reference influence our way of thinking in much broader way. It is important to know that frame of reference can change of way of decision making and thinking.

What is Technology?

Technology is practical set of knowledge, skills and experience through which human has change and use environment to create tools, machine that ease our life or meets our desires. Different set of people have different ideas about what technology means. All the tools that have ease our lives from beginning of the civilization far before that in ancient times to the technologically advance today’s new world are milestones of our technology.

But what about your dumb brain?

It is no brainer what technology is but most people get confuse about technology today.

Moore’s Law refers to Moore’s perception that the number of transistors on a microchip doubles every two years, though the cost of computers is halved.

But today’s world is more about social life side of technology which makes you dumb in sense which rewires your brain to act dumb. As our technological side get lit up by our online presence our thinking also changes. Today’s generation is always online generation, it was and it will be there is no denying to this.   As we are seeking for online longer presence on the other hand we are unable to remember phone numbers. Tell me last time when did you made phone call to your friend only using dialer. That’s not it tell me last time you were without your smartphone for 5 minutes. This kind of technological evolution has great effect on our lives. We just can’t live without keeping those techy-things which are like drugs.

Even though today’s education wants to go digital and students are consuming the educational content but is it making any sense to their brains? Technology has great potential to make students smarter but in most schools we are crippling the technology in ways that hold students back. What we make allow them to do? Let’s copy from here and paste it in their projects. We make them only read and not to think. To make realization that technology can make greater impact on lives of students we need to add technology in education in such way that it will be beneficial. 

Andrew Keen, author of The Internet is Not the Answer, says:

“technology is making us shallower thinkers, multi-tasking, unable to digest speeches, even songs, perpetually flicking”

We only remember what we pay attention to but in in this online party where you’re in constant fear of FOMO that we are constantly scrolling through our social media apps. The available information on internet is huge and sometimes cannot be comprehend. In this we lose some important memories and also always being online and looking for information does not create permanent memories. 

In every sense technology has crippled our way of thinking and making business decisions. It can be impossible to think critically or maybe we don’t even do that anymore. In business only way to expand is thinking that surely has to be done. And that is why entrepreneurs can create a solution for problem which no one can see or you might be too lazy for that.

Conclusion

The role and impact of technology in both our personal and working lives is ever growing. Understanding how people shape technology and how technology shapes people’s interactions with each other and the natural world is important not only for those who research, develop and implement new technologies but also for all those people and organisations that have to use those technologies in their working and personal lives. Still I am uncertain is technology making me smart or dumb? What do you think?
